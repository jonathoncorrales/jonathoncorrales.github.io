Biography

        My career as a graphic designer began when I began attending Miami Arts Studio (back when it was still called Zelda Glazer and was only a middle school). My first year I took a class which I believed would only teach me how to type efficiently. Little did I know we would delve into Photoshop that very year. I decided to continue with the class, and currently it is my fifth year taking it. Throughout the years I have refined my skills, slowly but definitely improving.


        Along with learning digital design in programs such as Photoshop, Illustrator, and various other programs, through my peers and assignments entitled E-Journals and Webliographies, I have discovered a passion 

for computer engineering and computer science. This discovery has greatly affected me, causing me to be informed about the newest technology, inducing me to build a computer myself, and even dictating the career path I have chosen to follow, 


        I'd like to complete a master's degree in Computer Science at a technology centered university, and apply for a job at a technology based corporation. These are my academic and professional goals, my life will not center around these. Aside from taking part in such a corporation, I would like to freelance. Because of my knowledge and training in digital design, I believe that, even currently, I make some impressive work that 

 is worthy of being sold, and with more training, I could become truly great and even possibly start a side-business. 

    

        Since even before I began attending Miami Arts Studio, I had an interest in video games, and as my interest for computer hardware increased, so did my interest/joy for video gaming. I became and avid gamer, but I didn't only care about what was the most "fun". I was interested in new, upcoming  ideas, indie or popular. I was interested in what was revolutionizing the industry. This is why I'd like to learn all aspects of game creation and one day create a game entirely by myself. However this would remain on the side lines, as my hobby.
